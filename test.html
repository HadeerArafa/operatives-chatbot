Petty Cash Funds

\n

Petty cash funds are small amounts of cash kept on hand to cover minor expenses that are not practical to pay by check or credit card. Here's a detailed breakdown of how petty cash funds work in Oracle Payables:

\n

Setting Up Petty Cash Funds

\n
    \n
  1. Designate a Petty Cash Administrator: Choose a responsible employee to manage the petty cash fund. This person will be responsible for receiving the initial fund, making disbursements, and reconciling the fund.
    2. \n
  2. Establish a Petty Cash Account: Set up a separate general ledger account specifically for petty cash. This account will be used to track all petty cash transactions.
    3. \n
  3. Determine the Fund Amount: Decide on the initial amount of cash to be kept in the petty cash fund. This amount should be sufficient to cover anticipated minor expenses.
    4. \n
  4. Create a Petty Cash Request: The petty cash administrator submits a request to the accounts payable department for the initial fund amount. This request should include necessary approvals.
    5. \n
  5. Enter the Petty Cash Request as an Invoice: The request is entered into Oracle Payables as a standard invoice. The amount is charged to the petty cash GL account. It's recommended to use a standard invoice naming convention (e.g., "IPC" for "Initiate Petty Cash" and the date) to help with research and reconciliation.
    6. \n
  6. Pay the Petty Cash Administrator: The accounts payable department issues a payment to the petty cash administrator for the fund amount.
    7. \n
\n

Managing Petty Cash

\n
    \n
  1. Disbursements: The petty cash administrator makes payments from the fund for approved expenses. They should keep a detailed log of all disbursements, including the date, amount, purpose, and supporting documentation (receipts).
    2. \n
  2. Reconciliation: The petty cash administrator periodically reconciles the fund. This involves comparing the cash on hand with the disbursement log. Any discrepancies should be investigated and corrected.
    3. \n
  3. Replenishment: When the petty cash fund falls below a predetermined threshold, the administrator submits a request for replenishment. This request is processed like the initial fund request.
    4. \n
\n

Key Benefits of Petty Cash Funds

\n
    \n
  • Convenience: Petty cash funds provide a quick and easy way to pay for small expenses.
    • \n
  • Efficiency: They eliminate the need for multiple small checks or credit card transactions.
    • \n
  • Control: Proper management of petty cash funds helps ensure that expenses are properly documented and accounted for.
    • \n
\n

Important Considerations

\n
    \n
  • Security: Petty cash funds should be kept in a secure location and access should be restricted.
    • \n
  • Documentation: All disbursements should be properly documented with receipts and a detailed log.
    • \n
  • Regular Reconciliation: Regular reconciliation is essential to prevent discrepancies and ensure accurate accounting.
    • \n
\n

By following these steps, you can effectively implement and manage petty cash funds in your organization using Oracle Payables.
